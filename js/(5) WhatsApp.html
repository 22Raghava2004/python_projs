  1. Introduction to the Rankine Cycle
 The Rankine cycle is a fundamental thermodynamic cycle used to convert heat into mechanical work, which is then used to generate electricity. It is widely used in thermal power plants that utilize sources such as coal, nuclear, geothermal, biomass, and solar energy. The Rankine cycle is a practical version of the Carnot cycle, which is the most efficient theoretical heat engine cycle.
